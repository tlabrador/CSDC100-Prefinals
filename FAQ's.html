What is Generative AI?

For instance, when you want to hop on a trend where you ask an AI tool to generate you an image where you're in one frame with your kpop idol, to be specific on a polariod with them, or if you prompt an AI to create a poem for your school project. That's generative AI making the magic for you. Generative AI is a type of artificial intelligence tool that generates images, text, videos, and other media in response to inputted prompts from a user. (Coursera)

How does it work?

Generative AI models generates new content by using neural networks (a type of machine learning algorithm designed to function as an artificial human brain) to identify patterns in existing data. Trained on unsupervised and semi-supervised learning approaches, organizations can create foundation models from large, unlabeled data sets, essentially forming a base for AI systems to perform tasks. (Coursera)

While ChatGPT is one of the most well-known generative AI tools, there are many others that have emerged as equal competitors. There's COPILOT which helps with writing, coding, and productivity. What can Generative AI do?

Generative AI can do so much, making our daily lives more efficient and convenient. It allows us to create content, solve problems, and access information quickly, turning tasks that used to take hours into things we can do almost instantly, right at our fingertips.
Here are some things AI can do according to the GeeksforGeeks :

Content Creation

1. Text: Writing articles, stories, poetry, and reports. AI can help draft and improve content.
2. Images: Creating art, graphics, and marketing visuals from text descriptions.
3. Music: Composing songs, background music, and sound effects.
4. Video: Making videos, animations, and enhancing editing.

Design and Art

1. Graphic Design: Creating logos, posters, and visual content with AI suggestions.
2. Fashion: Designing clothes, patterns, and styles.
3. Interior Design: Making virtual room layouts and suggesting decor ideas.

Healthcare

1. Drug Discovery: Designing molecules and predicting their effects.
2. Medical Imaging: Improving and analyzing medical images, creating training data.
3.Personalized Medicine: Suggesting treatment plans based on patient data.

Business and Marketing

1. Chatbots & Assistants: AI responds naturally to customer queries.
2. Market Analysis: Producing reports and insights from data.
3. Personalized Marketing: Creating customized content and product suggestions.

Education

1. Tutoring: Offering personalized lessons and answers.
2. Content Generation: Making exercises, quizzes, and learning materials.
3. Language Translation: Translating text and helping language learning.

Limitations or Risks of Generative AI

Despite AI's strengths and its great contribution to our daily lives. Generative AI still have some risks and limitations that we need to look out for in order for us to use it ethically and responsibly. Here are some limitations of generative AI according to Coursera and Microsoft:

Requires Human Oversight

GenAI can make mistakes, lacks understanding and empathy, so humans must fact-check its outputs.

Bias in training data

AI systems learn from existing human-created content. AI can inherit societal biases from the data it learns from.

Privacy concerns

AI systems store user interactions and inputs, and sensitive personal or business information may be stored or exposed.

Lack of transparency

It’s difficult to understand how outputs are generated,. This hinders the ability to hold AI accountable.

Intellectual property issues

Ethical concerns regarding authorship, ownership, and accountability are an ongoing debate in the realm of AI and remainsS unresolved.
